Teaching

During my studies at ETH Zurich, I was highly involved in teaching.

Algorithms and Data Structures

Topics: Introduction to algorithm analysis (runtime, asymptotic notation, induction, divide-and-conquer, recursion), Sorting and Searching Algorithms, Dynamic Programming, basic Graph algorithms (DFS, BFS, Topological sorting, algorithms for the shortest path problem, minimum spanning trees, ...), Data Structures (queue, stack, heaps, binary search tree, AVL trees, red-black trees)

My responsibilities included:

  • Helding weekly exercise session for about 25 students
  • Grading homeworks
  • Assisting the professor during the lectures
  • Preparing a summary to help the Students

Algorithms and Probability

Topics: Graph Theory (Eulerian/ Hamilton paths, Matching, Coloring, Articulation nodes, flows in networks, ...), Introduction to Probability Theory (conditional probability, independence, Bayes formula, random variables, Chernoff bounds, ...), Las Vegas and Monte Carlo algorithms, Selection of randomized algorithms (hashing, short longest paths, Min Cuts, Smallest Enclosing Circle, Jarvis' Wrap, ...)

My responsibilities included:

  • Helding weekly exercise session for about 25 students
  • Grading homeworks
  • Assisting the professor during the lectures
  • Preparing quizzes and mini-tests
  • Preparing a summary to help the Students
